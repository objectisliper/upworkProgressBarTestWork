WHAT IS SOCIAL ENGINEERING?

Social engineering is a non-technical attack strategy that relies heavily on human interaction. It is based on exploiting human psychology by manipulating people to incautiously provide confidential data, unknowingly infect their computers with malware, or open links to infected sites. Criminals use social engineering tactics because it is usually easier to exploit usersâ€™ weaknesses than it is to find a network or software vulnerability.

For example, instead of trying to find a software glitch, a social engineer might call an employee and pose as an IT support person, trying to trick the employee into revealing their company login credentials.
