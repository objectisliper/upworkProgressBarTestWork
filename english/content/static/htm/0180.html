LESSON 5: SECURE INTERNET USAGE

Cards  
Points  

The Internet has revolutionized the way we live our lives. However, there are a number of risks associated with going online. These result from either unknowingly visiting malicious websites or inadvertent disclosure of personal information.

For example, if you use the Internet, your browser keeps a record in its ‘history’ of which sites you have visited. Every user who has access to your PC can view your surfing history if they know how to access it. Unsafe surfing can also lead to other threats—from embarrassing personal comments to images that, once online, are nearly impossible to erase. It is a great temptation to quickly write a comment about a product, newspaper article, or other event on the Internet. Unfortunately, the Internet doesn't forget anything. And later you might regret it, if people find these posts online.

On the following slide, Susan John presents some tips to minimize the risks when surfing the Internet.

Tips for surfing the internet safely

TIP
#1/11

Avoid clicking on everything

There are very few ways for you to get your device infected with malware online, that is, UNLESS you click on it yourself. This means you should avoid clicking on banners advertising offers that are too good to be true. Also, be cautious of misleading pop-ups.

TIP
#2/11

Be selective with your downloads

Downloading new programs for every task you want to complete increases the likelihood that you will stumble on something malicious. Be careful what software you download.

TIP
#3/11

Only download from trusted sources

When you need to download new software, always try the developer’s site first. This is safer than using third-party download service providers.

TIP
#4/11

Always pay attention to website names

Attackers often adopt very similar spellings for their malicious websites to trick users into believing they are visiting a legitimate site. For instance, a site might ask you to make a payment to paypol.com, conveniently including a direct link to that site. Of course, this is NOT a website connected to PayPal.

TIP
#5/11

Don’t open any file that you are uncertain about

Even if you happen to download a file infected with a virus or worm, this doesn’t mean your system is in any real danger. That is, unless you run the file. So, make sure you don’t open any file that you don’t fully trust until you can safely confirm its integrity – or delete it.

TIP
#6/11

Look at the file extension

Be very cautious with executable files. Attackers often disguise malicious files under fake extensions in order to trick you. Examples are “.txt.vb” or “.jpg.exe”.

TIP
#7/11

Don’t give out your email address

When websites ask for your email address, be cautious. This could be a clever way to spam your email will all kinds of malicious messages. When optional, don’t provide your email address.

TIP
#8/11

Be cautious when using public computers

Many websites will not automatically log you off when you close the browser. This puts your personal information at risk if you use a public computer and someone else sits down after you. So, always make sure to log out of any and all accounts if you’re not using your personal or company issued devices.

TIP
#9/11

Use encrypted websites when possible

It is best to use https:// websites whenever possible as they offer a secure connection. Never send bank info, credit card numbers, confidential email messages, or other sensitive data unless you’re sure you’re on a secure site.

TIP
#10/11

Verify the security status of your Internet connection

People don’t realize that public Wi-Fi networks – such as those offered at coffee shops, airports, and hotels – are often very risky for doing important things like email, banking, and social networking. Whether you’re using a smartphone, laptop, tablet, or a public computer, it is important to connect only to a network you trust.

TIP
#11/11

Hover over website links

Take your cursor over any link to reveal its true destination. Look for links that, when you hover over them, show a different destination than the one you see in the text.
