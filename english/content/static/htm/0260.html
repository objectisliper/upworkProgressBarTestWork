LESSON 9: SECURITY INCIDENTS

Cards  
Points  

Due to the increasingly complex and growing importance of IT structures in all areas, threats from crime and misconduct are also increasing. For this reason, security incidents that threaten the availability, authenticity or integrity of data and systems or cause the loss of confidentiality must be dealt with quickly and effectively.

But what are actually typical security incidents? Let Susan John explain you on the board.

What are security incidents?

  • Security vulnerabilities in software
  • Targeted hacker attacks
  • Malware such as viruses or Trojans
  • Phishing attempts
  • Misconfigurations that allow access to confidential data
  • Misconduct of users
  • Insufficient protection of premises with sensitive hardware or other infrastructure
  • Loss of a mobile device (Notebook or Smartphone)

How to deal with security incidents?

TIP
#1/4

Don’t wait – report incidents immediately!

If your IT equipment is lost or stolen, contact the Service Desk immediately so that the necessary action can be taken immediately.

TIP
#2/4

Know who to contact

Contact the Service Desk if you are confronted with malware alerts.

TIP
#3/4

Don’ try to solve the issue yourself!

Do not take hasty measures yourself, but report issues to the Service Desk immediately. This will actively help to protect the data from misuse and avoid greater damage.

TIP
#4/4

Don’t involve anyone else!

Do not give any information about security incidents to individuals who are not involved.
