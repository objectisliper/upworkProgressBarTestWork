Points  

Risks related to social media

SPAM

Crafty spammers take advantage of the popularity of social networks to design new spamming techniques week after week.

SEO ATTACKS

The SEO idea is simple and involves utilizing optimal keywords and links in a way that ranks a website very high and displays it among the top search results. Attackers use best SEO strategies on websites and social networks to gain higher ranking for seemingly relevant keywords and topics and thus trick users to click on their links and end up on their pages.

Scammers know that society has unconsciously placed a pressure on people to get as many friends or followers on social media as possible. Consequently, cyber criminals have learned to leverage on this to get people’s account names and passwords, promising new followers daily.

IMPERSONATION OF CELEBRITIES AND FRIENDS

Scammers find it quite easy to register new accounts using celebrities’ alias and publicly available pictures. These accounts primarily contain advertisement and SPAM links.

PHISHING

Phishing attacks are very common on social media platforms and come in many different flavors. Typically, the user receives a message containing a link to an ‘interesting’ picture and the question: “Is this you on this photo?” The landing page for that link is a phishing page that intends to steal the user’s login credentials for the given social network.

APPS AND WIDGETS

Some social networks allow active content in the form of applications or widgets to be embedded in their message forms. These applications can then interact with the user and his/her group of friends. A simple example would be a daily joke application which posts new humorous content to the user’s profile page. Some social networks allow the inclusion of remote code which poses a great risk as it is harder to control what will be loaded.

LESSON 6: Secure Social Media Usage / TIPs

Cards  
Points  

A few tips for secure social media usage

TIP
#1/8

Think twice before you post

Once a message is published, it is nearly impossible to completely purge it from the corresponding social network platform. Therefore, think twice before posting pictures you wouldn’t want your employers, parents, or partner to see. Protect your reputation on all social networks.

TIP
#2/8

Don’t share sensitive data!

A status post like “Two days to go to the one-week family vacation. Disney World, here we come!” might seem relatively harmless, but you have just told all your friends, as well as all their friends, that you will be away from home for a full week. This type of message is comparable to placing a signpost on the road to your house, reading, “Empty House,” for all to see.

TIP
#3/8

Know what action to take

If someone is harassing or threatening you, remove them from your friends list, block them, and report them to the site administrator.

TIP
#4/8

Be cautious when talking about your job or company

Be very careful what kind of personal information you share online, especially when it concerns your job. For example, the statement “My boss just laid off 30 employees” can have serious public relation and financial consequences for the company. The impact can be even more serious if the organization is publicly owned.

TIP
#5/8

Avoid reusing passwords in any way

It can be quite difficult to generate and remember unique, strong, robust passwords. Still, it is crucial to not reuse your passwords among different platforms. Also, change your passwords regularly and use multi-factor authentication when available.

TIP
#6/8

Curate your connections

The more connections you have, the higher the possibility of befriending a fraudulent or compromised account that will send you a malicious link. Trim your contact list and keep only those connections that are professional colleagues or personal friends.

TIP
#7/8

Use different profile photos across social media accounts

Remember that images can easily be found via search engines. Therefore, utilize unique profile photos to differentiate between your personal and professional accounts and change them on a regular basis.

TIP
#8/8

Use social media privacy settings

Spend a few minutes to tighten the privacy settings on your social media profiles as this strategy is safer than the default settings offered by most such platforms.
